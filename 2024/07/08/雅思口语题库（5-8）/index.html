Do you want to go into outer space in the future?

  • I don’t have interest in going into outer space.
  • Outer space is cold and dark, and there is not even oxygen. (客观描述)
  • I am very afraid to go into such a dangerous environment. (主观感受)

这是一个很好的观察。在英语中,用”interest”表示”兴趣”时,有两种常见的用法:

  1. 带有不定冠词”a”或”an”:
    • I have a strong interest in history.
    • She developed a keen interest in cooking.

在这种用法中,”a/an interest”强调的是某种特定的兴趣或感兴趣的事物。

  1. 不带冠词:
    • I have interest in learning new languages.
    • She showed great interest in the project.

在这种用法中,”interest”作为抽象名词,表示一种广泛的或一般的兴趣。

通常来说,当我们想强调某种具体的兴趣时,会使用”a/an interest”。而当我们想表达更一般性的兴趣时,则会直接使用”interest”。

两种用法都是正确的,区别在于要强调的重点不同。选择哪种方式取决于语境和要传达的具体意思。

参考句：

  • It opens our eyes to the interesting aspects of chemistry

  • One of the most interesting old persons I have met is my chemistry teacher, Mr. Johnson. I first encountered him on my very first day of high school, walking into the chemistry lab with a mix of excitement and nervousness. I was immediately struck by his welcoming demeanor and the infectious energy he brought into the classroom.

    Mr. Johnson is in his late 70s, and his passion for chemistry is as vibrant as it was when he first started teaching over 50 years ago. He has a wealth of knowledge and experience, having taught countless students throughout his career. His kind nature is evident in how he interacts with us; he takes the time to get to know each student, and he always makes a point to encourage anyone who might be struggling.

    What makes Mr. Johnson particularly interesting is his unique approach to teaching. He frequently incorporates humor into his lessons, using funny anecdotes and clever jokes to explain complex concepts. His playful manner not only makes learning enjoyable but also helps to create a relaxed environment where students feel comfortable asking questions. He often uses real-life examples to relate chemistry to the world around us, making the subject feel relevant and fascinating.

    Beyond just being a teacher, Mr. Johnson is a person of deep curiosity and a lifelong learner. He is always up to date with the latest developments in science and is eager to share this knowledge with his students. What truly sets him apart, however, is his ability to inspire a love for chemistry in others. Many of his former students have gone on to pursue careers in science, crediting his teaching style and support as pivotal influences in their lives.

    I find Mr. Johnson incredibly interesting not only because of his expertise in chemistry, but also due to his ability to connect with students on a personal level. His kindness, humor, and genuine passion for teaching have left a lasting impression on me. He embodies the idea that education is not just about imparting knowledge, but about inspiring curiosity and fostering a love for learning. Meeting him has truly enriched my high school experience and influenced the way I view education and mentorship.
